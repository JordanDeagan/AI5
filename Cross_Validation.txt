3) Since leave-one-out cross validation selects 1 point to test with and trains on the rest of the data, whichever side the test point comes from will be smaller, meaning the other side will be the majority, and what the Majority Classifier will classify the test point as. If a positive point is left out for testing, there will be 100 negative points and 99 positive points. When Majority Classifier looks at the new point, which is positive, it will immediately put it in the majority, which is the negatives.
